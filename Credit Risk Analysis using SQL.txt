Problem: Analyze borrower data to understand:
default rates
income impact on default
loan distribution

Dataset:
Source: Kaggle (Credit Risk Dataset) 
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/laotse/credit-risk-dataset
Contains borrower financial details

What I Did:
Calculated overall default rate
Analyzed income vs default probability
Created income buckets
Evaluated loan distribution


Key Insight:
Loan percent income (debt burden) is the strongest predictor of default in this dataset, showing the highest separation in default rates across segments.

Past default remains an important secondary predictor, as it increases default probability even within high-risk groups.

Interest rate also captures risk, likely reflecting lender assessment of borrower risk, though its predictive strength is lower than loan burden.
While high interest rates indicate higher individual default risk, the medium interest segment represents a larger share of total portfolio risk due to higher borrower concentration.

Home ownership and income show weaker and less consistent separation, likely acting as proxy indicators of financial stability rather than primary drivers.
